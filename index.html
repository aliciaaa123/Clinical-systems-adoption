Root Cause Analysis

5 Key Findings From the Field

Finding 1
Training Design Mismatch
The 8-hour linear classroom model did not reflect the interrupt-driven, multi-patient reality of ED workflows. Staff felt underprepared for real-world conditions.
Finding 2
Super User Attrition
In the ED, 9 of 12 designated super users have returned to full clinical assignments. Only 3 remain — collapsing the first line of informal peer support.
Finding 3
Workaround Proliferation
6 of 10 Oncology nurses developed paper/whiteboard workarounds for medication reconciliation — creating a patient safety risk and degrading data quality.
Finding 4
Physician Engagement Gap
In 12 observed interactions, 7 involved verbal orders entered by nurses on behalf of physicians — distorting the audit trail and increasing nursing burden by ~35%.
Finding 5
Mobile Access Barriers
Oncology has 6 workstations-on-wheels for 22 nurses per shift. This hardware constraint is the second most cited adoption barrier — and is actively driving workarounds.

Pillar Detail

Intervention Actions & Owners

🛡️ Stabilize — Days 1–30

Action 1.1 · Days 1–7
Super User Reactivation Program
Negotiate a protected 4-hour/week schedule block for all super users — out of full patient assignment. Issue laminated "Quick Wins" tip cards covering the top 12 repeat ticket workflows.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + Nursing Manager Metric: All 12 ED super users re-engaged in 7 days
Action 1.2 · Days 1–10
At-the-Elbow Support Surge
10-day intensive ATE deployment in ED and Oncology. Adoption Specialist alternates day and evening shifts to observe real-time workflow gaps. Structured observation log captures recurring barriers.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + IT Help Desk Metric: 25% reduction in repeat ticket categories by Day 10
Action 1.3 · Days 3–14
Self-Service Knowledge Base
Department-branded Epic Quick Reference Library on the hospital intranet — organized by role and workflow (not Epic module name). Each article under 2 minutes to consume. Top 12 topics published first.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + IT Communications Metric: 200+ accesses in 30 days; 40% ticket reduction by Day 30

🔧 Reinforce — Days 15–45

Action 2.1 · Days 15–45
Micro-Learning Sprint Series
Replace failed 8-hour block model with 5 targeted 20-minute sessions delivered at point-of-care during shift transitions (7:00–7:30 AM & PM). Co-facilitated by Adoption Specialist and unit super user.
Owner: Adoption Specialist Metric: 90% attendance; post-session competency ≥ 80%

Action 2.2 · Days 15–60
Physician-Specific Engagement Program
1:1 Optimization Sessions — 30 min private coaching, no group judgment.   Dragon/Voice Dictation — setup and workflow integration where licensed.   Physician Champions — identify 2–3 early-proficient physicians per department to model best practices for peers.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + CMO + Medical Directors Metric: Physician self-entry ≥ 85%; doc completion ≥ 80% by Day 60
Action 2.3 · Days 15–45
Workaround Elimination Campaign
Three-step protocol for every identified workaround: 1. Understand — interview staff to find the exact friction point (no shame). 2. Match — identify the Epic feature that solves the same need. 3. Demonstrate — side-by-side demo + 72-hour follow-up.
Metric: Zero paper med-rec workarounds in Oncology by Day 45

⚡ Optimize — Days 30–75

Action 3.1 · Days 30–60
Personalized Epic Workspace Optimization ("15-Minute Tune-Up")
Individualized sessions reviewing and optimizing each staff member's Epic environment. Targets: SmartPhrases & order favorites for MDs; MAR preferences & Storyboard layouts for RNs; scheduling workflows for MAs & clerks.
Metric: Average doc time reduced ≥ 20% from baseline by Day 60
Action 3.2 · Days 20–30 build, then biweekly
Data Feedback Loop for Managers
Biweekly adoption dashboard built in Epic reporting and shared with each department manager — showing doc completion times, provider order compliance, ticket trends, and template utilization by unit and role.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + IT Analytics Metric: 2+ managers independently initiating staff adoption conversations by Day 60
Action 3.3 · Days 30–75
Oncology Specialty Workflow Optimization Review
Structured review with Oncology nursing leadership and Epic build team to address chemotherapy protocols, complex care plans, and MDT notes. Top 5 friction workflows identified → proposed enhancements → build tickets → validated with frontline staff before rollout.
Metric: ≥ 3 Oncology-specific enhancements implemented by Day 75

🌱 Sustain — Days 60–90

Action 4.1 · First meeting Day 60, then monthly
Super User Community of Practice
60-minute cross-departmental monthly forum for super users to share tips, escalate unsolved problems, and receive Epic enhancement updates. Creates horizontal knowledge flow and sustains super user identity beyond go-live.
Metric: ≥ 70% super user attendance at first 3 CoP sessions
Action 4.2 · Days 45–75
New Staff Onboarding Integration
Epic adoption training embedded into HR onboarding — not a separate optional event. Includes: pre-hire async e-learning, 4-hour live scenario-based training on Day 1 clinical orientation, super user floor walkthrough on Day 3, and 30-day Adoption Specialist check-in.
Metric: 100% of new hires completing all Epic onboarding touchpoints within 30 days
Action 4.3 · Day 90, then quarterly
Adoption Scorecard & Quarterly Leadership Review
Formal quarterly scorecard presented to Department Directors and the CMIO — consolidating compliance rates, ticket trends, documentation quality, training completion, and staff satisfaction scores. Transforms adoption from a one-time project into an ongoing operational discipline.
Owner: Adoption Specialist + Clinical Informatics + IT Analytics Metric: All departments ≥ 75% compliance by Day 90

Resistance Management

Treat Resistance as Information, Not Insubordination

🤨
The Skeptic
"This system doesn't work for how we actually practice."
The most credible response is action, not training. When a skeptic raises a legitimate concern, document it, escalate to the build team, and follow up with the outcome. Skeptics who see their feedback result in real system changes become the most powerful adoption advocates in the department.
😓
The Overwhelmed
"I don't have time to learn this right now."
Respect the clinical load. Offer micro-sessions (10–15 min), bring training to staff during natural downtime (post-handoff, lunch), and make clear the goal is to make their day easier — not to add to it.
📄
The Workaround Defender
"My paper system is faster."
Never argue with this perception — it may be accurate short-term. Instead, commit to making the Epic workflow faster than the paper alternative through workspace optimization and process redesign. Then deliver on that commitment.

Applicability to Boston Medical Center

Why This Approach Fits BMC

BMC operates as a safety-net hospital serving a high-complexity, high-vulnerability patient population. The clinical workflows — spanning inpatient, outpatient, behavioral health, and community health — demand an adoption approach built on four principles.

⚖️
Equity-Informed
Staff serving underserved populations carry heavier administrative burdens. Every adoption intervention actively reduces — never adds to — that burden.
🌍
Culturally Humble
BMC's diverse workforce requires role-appropriate, accessible training materials that avoid jargon assuming prior EHR familiarity.
🏥
Operationally Grounded
Every training intervention is anchored in a real observed workflow — not a theoretical use case. Abstract training disconnected from patient care doesn't work.
🤝
Collaborative
The Adoption Specialist role is a partnership function — with nursing, medicine, IT, informatics, and operations. No single person drives adoption alone.

Supporting Materials

Designed for laminated pocket card distribution to all super users.

Issue #1
Patient not found in Epic
Search by MRN, not name. Check "Include Discharged" filter.
Issue #2
Medication not in MAR
Verify order was activated; check pharmacy verification status.
Issue #3
Unable to sign note
Check for incomplete required fields (red asterisks); verify attending co-signature setup.
Issue #4
Lab results not showing
Check collection date filter; labs may be in "Resulted" inbox if unacknowledged.
Issue #5
Discharge summary not generating
Confirm all required encounter data fields complete; use Discharge Navigator.
Issue #6
Order not routing to pharmacy
Verify order was "signed" not just "entered"; check department routing preferences.
Issue #7
Patient on wrong unit
Census management — contact charge nurse or unit clerk for bed move in Epic.
Issue #8
Can't find a SmartForm/template
Use Epic search bar (magnifying glass icon); check specialty-specific template library.
Issue #9
Dragon dictation not working
Check microphone input device; Dragon service may need restart — contact IT.
Issue #10
Duplicate patient record
Do NOT merge — flag immediately to Health Information Management (HIM).
Issue #11
After Visit Summary not printing
Check printer assignment in workstation settings; use Print Preview first.
Issue #12
"No Access" error on patient
Verify patient is on your assigned panel/unit; may require Break-the-Glass access with documented justification.
